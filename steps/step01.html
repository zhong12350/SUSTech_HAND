Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Prepare Components

Gather all components listed in the tools and materials section. Lay them out on a clean, well-lit workspace.

Tip: Use an anti-static mat when handling electronic components to prevent damage from static electricity.
2

Identify Orientation Marks

Examine each component for orientation marks, notches, or alignment indicators. Refer to Figure 1.1 for reference.

Important: Incorrect orientation may prevent proper assembly or damage components.
3

Begin Assembly

Start assembling components as shown in Figure 1.2. Apply gentle pressure and ensure proper alignment before securing connections.

Tool Note: Use the appropriate screwdriver size to avoid stripping screw heads.
4

Secure Connections

Tighten all connections firmly but not excessively. Over-tightening can damage threads or crack components.

Proper Torque: Tighten until snug, then give an additional quarter turn.
5

Verify Assembly

Compare your assembly with Figure 1.5. Check all connections, alignments, and clearances.

Quality Check: Perform a visual inspection from multiple angles to ensure everything is properly assembled.
6

Test Functionality (If Applicable)

If this step involves moving parts or electronics, perform a basic functionality test before proceeding.

Test Protocol: Apply power briefly and check for smooth operation without binding or unusual noises.

Technical Notes

Tolerance Specifications: All 3D printed parts are designed with a 0.2mm tolerance. If parts don't fit, check for printing defects or excess material.

Torque Specifications: M3 screws should be tightened to 0.6-0.8 N·m. Avoid over-tightening to prevent stripping threads in plastic parts.

Electrical Safety: Always disconnect power when making or modifying electrical connections. Double-check polarity before applying power.

Testing Protocol: After assembly, perform a full range of motion test without load before applying operational forces.
