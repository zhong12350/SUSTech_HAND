Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Prepare Components

Gather all components listed in the tools and materials section. Lay them out on a clean, well-lit workspace.

Tip: Use an anti-static mat when handling electronic components to prevent damage from static electricity.
2

Identify Orientation Marks

Examine each component for orientation marks, notches, or alignment indicators. Refer to Figure 1.1 for reference.

Important: Ensure all components are oriented correctly before proceeding to avoid assembly issues.
3

Connect Tendons to Servo

Carefully connect the tendons to the servo as shown in the images. Make sure the connections are secure and follow the correct routing.

Tip: Apply a small amount of lubricant to the tendon paths to reduce friction and improve movement smoothness.
4

Test Servo Movement

Before final assembly, test the servo movement by manually rotating the gears. Ensure there is no binding or excessive friction.

Note: Proper tendon routing and servo alignment are crucial for optimal hand movement.
5

Secure Tendons and Gears

Once satisfied with the movement, secure the tendons and gears in place. Use the appropriate screws and ensure they are tightened properly.

6

Final Assembly Check

Perform a final check of all connections, alignments, and securements. Ensure the hand functions correctly before considering the assembly complete.

Tools Required

Screwdriver Set

For securing screws on the servo and other components.

Pliers

For bending and holding wires and small parts.

Craft Knife

For trimming and shaping components as needed.

Ruler or Caliper

For measuring and ensuring correct dimensions.

Materials Needed

Servo Motor 1
Tendons As needed

Assembly Notes

Ensure all components are free from dust and grease before assembly. Handle electronic components with care to avoid static damage.
