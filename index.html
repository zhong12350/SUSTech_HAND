Important Notice

The current SENS_HAND v1 release does not include sensors as we are still improving the reliability of the sensor version. The sensor version will be released later this year.

Before You Begin

Before starting assembly, make sure you have all necessary tools and parts ready:

  • Complete SENS_HAND v1 parts kit
  • Screwdriver set
  • Pliers
  • Tweezers
  • PTFE tubes and tendon wires

It's recommended to work on an anti-static mat and keep your workspace clean.

Ashley Stopper Knot Technique

The Ashley Stopper knot is widely used throughout the assembly process. This knot is the preferred connection method for robot tendon systems due to its reliability and easy identification.

Application Tendon End Fixation
Strength Level High
Reliability Excellent

Knot Tightening Operation Points

01

Bidirectional Even Tightening

Always firmly tighten the knot by pulling from both sides to ensure even tension distribution

02

Tool-Assisted Tensioning

Use pliers to apply additional tension to ensure the knot reaches optimal tightness

03

Reliability Check

Loose knots may come undone with repeated use. Always check tightness after each assembly

Critical Considerations

Dimensional Accuracy Requirement: Ensure the knot is tight, with approximately 0.5 cm distance between the knot and the end. This distance is crucial for ensuring tendon range of motion and avoiding interference.
